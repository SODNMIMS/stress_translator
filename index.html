About The Project

What does the project do?

Stress Translator is to show on board the average stress level of the class based on the number of assignments and their submission dates.

When the submission of an assignment is around a week away we are are chill about it. However, as the days go by and the submission comes closer we tend to get more and more tense. This product will represent your emotion in an emoji form as and when it changes.

Mission and Vision

Mission is to reduce the communication gap between students and teachers.

Vision is to ensure a healthy and stress free environment in classrooms across the globe one step at a time.

Ideatation

Ideation is the creative process of generating, developing, and communicating ideas for the project, where the ideas help us to understand the basic element of thought for the project. The aim of the ideation stage was to generate a large quantity of ideas that our team filtered and cut down into the best, most practical or most innovative ones in order to inspire new and better design solutions and products for the project. It helped us to get the obvious solutions out of our team’s heads, and it drove our team beyond these ideas.

Conceptualise

The purpose of conceptualisation was to improve our thoughts and take them to a higher level, so we could find gems within the frame of our minds. As a result of higher conceptualisation, we were able to come up with unique ideas. It encouraged us to make or see connections between things, to look for relationships and interdependencies. Such understandings were crucial to the Knowledge Process of Analysing functionally and provide a broader base for Analysing critically. Being able to Conceptualise with theory was essential for us to find existing solutions and technologies that could be incorporated in our idea to make it feasible.

Validation

Design validation was the process of evaluating the Javascript and Arduino Code during or at the end of the project development, it helped in ensuring the  system satisfies the specifications for the users. This was the stage where we actually build a version of the project and validated it against the user requirements.

Low Fidelity Prototype

Minimum Viable Product

- No more than 2 assignments per day (Official)
- Display emoji as per no.of days from next assignment
- Alert when the user adds on an assignment over the existing submission within two days through emojis (Unofficial)

The goal of the Low Fidelity Prototype was to turn our ideas into testable artifacts that we were then able to use to collect and analyze user’s feedback in the early stage. It also enabled us to gather user feedback on our sketched prototype. During this test, we were able to quickly redo the part of the design based on user’s comments in real time, in just a few minutes. This process helped us with early visualisation of alternative design solutions for our project, which helped in provoking innovative solutions and improvements. An additional advantage to this approach was that when we used rough sketches, users felt more comfortable to suggest changes.
